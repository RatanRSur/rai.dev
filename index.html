If it involves programming and hardware, I love it! These are the things I've been working on recently:
  • hackCooper is a hackathon organized by us at create@cooper and hosted at The Cooper Union. This Valentine's Day will be our second one (register). Last year, a huge proportion of the hacks were hardware hacks and I really want to double down on that this year. I think most students would enjoy staying up 24 hours building a funny robot more than they would the thousandth geotagging-social-sharing app.
  • Yorgo Alexopolous is an artist I'm programming for during the semester. The project I'm working on now involves a ton of cute hobbyist displays controlled by Raspberry Pi's and Arduinos. Some of the displays cycle through some pretty straightforward animations. Others would require a lot of thought to get a sense of what they're up to.
  • In the summertime I worked with openCV, a library of computer vision functions in C++, to create and improve edge detection algorithms for InfoInnovation. The algorithms were for use in prosthesis prototyping so the main issues were differentiation between the border of the hand and interior edges, accurate flesh detection and accurate finger detection.
